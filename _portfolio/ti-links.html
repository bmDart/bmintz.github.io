--- title: "" excerpt: "" collection: portfolio --- I've always been fascinated by Brunnian links. They have the peculiar property of falling apart if any individual component is removed. As such, they are a wonderful metaphor for the complex, fragile connections that keep this world together. The above picture is an infinite example based on a system I developped for constructing arbitrarily complex Brunnian links based on modules. Specifically, a swirl of four bands is mirrored in every other square of a tessellation. The system is described in a short paper for the 2024 Bridges Math Art Conference. Essentially, modules with a swirl of k bands are in bijection with vertices of degree k in a graph, so this system allows one to translate any graph into a Brunnian link!
